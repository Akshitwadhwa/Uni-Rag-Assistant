University Attendance and Assessment Policy

All undergraduate students must maintain a minimum of 75 percent attendance in each registered theory and laboratory course. Attendance is calculated separately for each course and not as a combined semester average.

Students with attendance between 65 percent and 74.99 percent may apply for condonation only in exceptional cases such as documented medical emergencies, university-approved sports participation, or bereavement in the immediate family. Condonation requires approval from the department chair and the dean of academics.

Students with attendance below 65 percent in any course are not eligible to appear for the end semester examination in that course. Such students must repeat the course in a future semester according to the backlog registration policy.

Internal assessment consists of:

- two mid-semester tests carrying 20 marks each
- quizzes and assignments carrying 20 marks
- attendance and class participation carrying 10 marks
- laboratory continuous evaluation carrying 30 marks for lab courses

To pass a theory course, a student must obtain at least 40 percent in the end semester examination and at least 50 percent overall when internal and external components are combined.
