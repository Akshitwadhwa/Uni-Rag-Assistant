B.Tech Computer Science and Engineering Curriculum Overview

The Bachelor of Technology in Computer Science and Engineering is an eight-semester program totaling 160 credits. The curriculum combines foundational mathematics, core computing subjects, laboratories, open electives, and a capstone project.

Semester 1 commonly includes:
- Calculus and Linear Algebra
- Engineering Physics
- Problem Solving using Python
- Basic Electronics
- Communication Skills

Semester 2 commonly includes:
- Discrete Mathematics
- Data Structures
- Digital Logic Design
- Object-Oriented Programming
- Environmental Studies

Semester 3 core courses include:
- Design and Analysis of Algorithms
- Database Management Systems
- Computer Organization and Architecture
- Operating Systems
- Statistics for Data Science

Semester 4 core courses include:
- Theory of Computation
- Computer Networks
- Software Engineering
- Web Technologies
- Artificial Intelligence Fundamentals

Students must complete at least one mini project before the end of the sixth semester and one major capstone project in the final year. Open electives may be chosen from data analytics, cloud computing, cybersecurity, natural language processing, and internet of things depending on seat availability.
